Abstract    :
              The OSU Campus Chemical Instrument Center will purchase a                      
              Fourier transform nuclear magnetic resonance spectrometer                      
              for use in structural analysis and mechanistic studies of                      
              proteins and enzymes (for example, alpha-lactalbumin, 14                       
              kDa; sulfite reductase, 24 kDa; ribonuclease H, 17 kDa; high                   
              potential iron protein, 9.5 kDa; adenylate kinase, 21.7 kDa;                   
              phospholipase A2, 14 kDa; catalytic domain of leader                           
              peptidase, 28 kDa; and flavodoxin, 16 kDa) and some                            
              synthetic polypeptides from immunogenic and long range                         
              electron-transfer studies.
