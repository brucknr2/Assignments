Abstract    :
               This project investigates the structure of semantics  definitions of
              programming languages.  First, models of the  ``core'' of a  programming
              language are analyzed and classified;  next, extensions of the core  are
              analyzed for their harmony  (correspondence) and orthogonality, and models  of
              these  extensions are related to each other and to models of the  core.  The 
              ultimate goal is a ``design and analysis workbench,''  in which a language can
              be  synthesized, classified, and  prototyped.  Such a workbench will aid
              language  designers and  provide experimental evidence that languages with
              harmonious  designs have clean and correct implementations.
