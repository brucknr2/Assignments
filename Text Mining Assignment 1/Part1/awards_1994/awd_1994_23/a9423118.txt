Abstract    :
                Questions concerning population origins and affinities associated with the
              colonization of the Pacific have long held the fascination of anthropologists,
              archaeologists, and linguists: the colonization of the Pacific has been
              described as one of the great adventures and accomplishments of humans. 
              Despite this intense interest in the story behind the settlement of the
              Pacific, many questions remain open concerning the source, number, and timing
              of migrations to various parts of the Pacific.  This project will employ the
              latest molecular techniques  to follow the genetic trail of ancient migrations
              out into the Pacific from Indonesia and Southeast Asia.  The group has had
              great success in discerning the movements and origins of Polynesians and their
              precursers, and more will follow with this intensive laboratory study.
