Abstract    :
                   This project focuses on the structure of complexity classes that  are 
              known to lie between the classes P and PSPACE, on the one  hand, and on the 
              circuit complexity classes, on the other hand.  Many of the well-studied 
              complexity classes can be specified by  considering computation trees and 
              circuits with functions of k-  valued logic.   It is of interest to learn the 
              power of this  method of specification; that is, what classes can or cannot be 
              characterized this way.  Specifically, while `leaf languages`  have been 
              successfully used in some settings, it is not known how  broadly they can be 
              used.  Similarly, the study of the structure  of the Boolean hierarchy or of
              the  polynomial hierarchy over a  complexity class of partitions may yield
              approaches  for studying  the Boolean hierarchy on NP or the polynomial-time
              hierarchy,  respectively.
