Abstract    :
                Interior-point methods have proven to be efficient for   solution of large,
              sparse linear and quadratic programming    problems.  These  methods are being
              extended to cover more general  classes of   problems.  In  particular,
              interior-point technology  are being used to   create efficient  algorithms for
              the solution of  convex programming problems   and semidefinite  programming 
              problems.  The first class is important for   general nonlinear  optimization
              and the second has important applications to   integer  programming.
